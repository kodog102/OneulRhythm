# Today Experience

This document defines the product experience for Today.

It is the North Star for every future user-facing surface that presents today's rhythm, including:

- Today Screen
- Live Activity
- Widget
- Apple Watch

This is a Product Experience document.

It is not a UI specification.

It is not a design system.

It does not define architecture.

It defines what should remain true for the next years of the product.

---

# Vision

Today exists to help people reconnect with the one rhythm that matters most right now.

It is not a task manager.

It is not a productivity dashboard.

It is not a place to organize the whole day.

People already chose today's rhythm.

Life becomes busy.

Attention drifts.

Today quietly restores awareness.

When someone opens Today, they should understand what deserves attention now.

They should complete the needed interaction quickly.

Then they should return to their day.

The application should not ask them to stay.

Presence belongs outside the app.

One rhythm.

One moment.

One gentle action.

Then return to life..

---

# Product Principles

## One Rhythm at a Time

Only one rhythm should hold primary attention.

Even when many rhythms exist in the day, the experience presents a single focus.

This principle exists because attention is limited.

A calm product reduces choice at the moment of use.

The application chooses what matters most right now.

The user does not need to decide which rhythm to look at first.

---

## Calm over Busy

Today should feel quiet.

It should never feel urgent, crowded, or demanding.

This principle exists because the product's purpose is presence, not pressure.

Busy interfaces create management.

Calm interfaces create recognition.

If the screen feels like work, the experience has failed.

---

## Gentle Progress

Progress should feel soft and respectful.

Completing a rhythm is a quiet acknowledgment, not a victory.

Leaving a rhythm incomplete is part of life, not a failure.

This principle exists because days change.

Unexpected moments happen.

The experience must never create guilt.

It must never ask the user to catch up.

---

## Minimal Decision Making

Today should require as few decisions as possible.

The user should rarely need to compare, prioritize, or configure while looking at Today.

This principle exists because decision fatigue is the opposite of rhythm.

The product remembers what the user already decided.

Today only asks for what cannot be known without the user: whether this rhythm has been completed.

---

## Today Only

Today concerns this day alone.

Yesterday is not reviewed here.

Tomorrow is not planned here.

This principle exists to protect presence.

Reflection may come later in the product's life.

Management belongs elsewhere.

Today's job is simple: stay with what matters now.

---

# Experience Goals

## Instant Orientation

Within one second, the user should understand today's focus.

They should not need to scan a list.

They should not need to interpret competing signals.

The primary rhythm should be immediately clear.

---

## Brief Interaction

Most interactions with Today should take only a few seconds.

Open.

Recognize.

Act, if needed.

Leave.

The experience succeeds when the user does not linger.

---

## Return to Life

Today should encourage returning to daily life.

The application is a brief companion, not a destination.

If the user stays inside the app longer than necessary, the experience is too heavy.

---

## Quiet Continuity

Throughout the day, the sense of today's rhythm should remain gentle and continuous.

Whether the user is inside the app or away from it, the meaning of "what matters now" should feel the same.

Surfaces may differ in size and timing.

They must not differ in meaning.

---

## Soft Language of State

The experience should speak gently about the day.

It should never frame the day as failed, missed, overdue, urgent, or warning.

Preferred feelings include:

- Current
- Next
- Past
- Continue
- Complete
- Quietly done

Words shape emotion.

Emotion shapes whether the product remains welcome.

# Information Hierarchy

Information should appear in this order of importance:

1. Primary Rhythm
2. Rhythm Meaning
3. Primary Action
4. Progress
5. Next Rhythm
6. Supporting InformationInformation

### Rhythm Meaning

The meaning of the current rhythm.

This explains why the rhythm deserves attention now.

Meaning gives emotional context before action.

The user should first understand what is happening,
then decide whether action is needed.

Without meaning, the experience becomes mechanical.

Without action, the experience can still succeed.

## Why this order exists

### Primary Rhythm

The single rhythm that deserves attention now.

Without this, Today has no center.

Everything else is secondary.

### Primary Action

The one action the user may take for the primary rhythm.

Usually, this means acknowledging completion.

The action should feel available, never insistent.

### Progress

A soft sense of how the day is moving.

Progress exists to reassure, not to score.

It should never dominate the primary rhythm.

### Next Rhythm

A quiet preview of what comes after.

It helps orientation without competing for attention.

It should never feel like a second task waiting to be managed.

### Supporting Information

Anything else: time context, lighter details, secondary cues.

These may help understanding.

They must never compete with the primary rhythm.

# State Philosophy

Today moves through emotional states across the day.

Each state has a feeling the product should protect.

## Empty

The day has no rhythm yet.

The feeling should be open, not broken.

Empty is an invitation to begin, not a problem to solve under pressure.

The experience should remain calm and light.

It should never make the absence of rhythm feel like failure.

---

## Upcoming

Rhythms exist, but none is current yet.

The feeling should be quiet anticipation.

The user should sense what comes next without being asked to prepare intensely.

Upcoming is waiting, not urgency.

---

## Current

A rhythm is happening now.

The feeling should be clear and grounded.

This is the heart of Today.

The user should recognize the rhythm immediately and know what, if anything, they may do.

Current should feel present, not alarming.

---

## Past Incomplete

A rhythm's time has passed, and it remains incomplete.

The feeling should be gentle continuation, never reproach.

Life moved on.

The rhythm may still be acknowledged if the user wishes.

It must never be framed as missed, overdue, or failed.

The product waits politely.

It does not push.

---

## Day Complete

The meaningful rhythms of the day have been completed.

The feeling should be quiet closure.

Not celebration.

Not a scoreboard.

Just a soft sense that today's rhythm has been honored.

Then the experience may become still.

The user should feel free to leave.

# Component Philosophy

These sections describe purpose, not layout.

## Primary Rhythm Area

Responsibility: hold the single focus of the day.

This area answers one question:

> What matters right now?

It should carry the emotional center of the screen.

Nothing else should outshine it.

---

## Primary Action Area

Responsibility: offer the one meaningful interaction for the primary rhythm.

This area exists so recognition can become acknowledgment.

It should feel easy and optional in spirit, even when available.

It should never nag.

---

## Progress Area

Responsibility: show gentle movement through the day.

This area exists to provide orientation, not motivation through pressure.

Progress should remain visually and emotionally quieter than the primary rhythm.

---

## Next Rhythm Area

Responsibility: softly indicate what follows.

This area exists to reduce uncertainty about the near future.

It should support calm continuity.

It should not create a second center of attention.

---

## Empty Guidance Area

Responsibility: help the user begin when no rhythm exists.

This area exists only when the day is empty.

Its tone should remain welcoming and light.

It should never turn emptiness into urgency.

---

## Day Complete Area

Responsibility: close the day gently.

This area exists when today's rhythm has been honored.

Its purpose is release, not reward.

The user should feel finished and free to return to life.

---

# What Should Always Remain True

- Today presents one primary focus at a time.
- The meaning of "what matters now" stays consistent across surfaces.
- Incomplete rhythms are treated with respect.
- Completing a rhythm feels soft.
- The user can understand the day quickly.
- The product never invents urgency.
- Today remains about presence, not management.

---

# What Should Never Happen

- Multiple rhythms competing for equal attention
- Dense dashboards or task lists as the primary experience
- Guilt, pressure, or catch-up language
- Repeated demands for the same acknowledgment
- Asking the user to plan tomorrow inside Today
- Making the app feel like a place to stay and manage
- Turning progress into a score, streak, or performance measure
- Making the user wonder what deserves attention first

---

# Relationship to Other Surfaces

Today Screen is the clearest expression of this experience.

Live Activity, Widget, and Apple Watch should carry the same meaning in smaller or more ambient forms.

They may show less.

They must not mean something different.

This document defines the shared experience philosophy.

It does not define the detailed behavior of each surface.

# Product Integrity

Every future feature should strengthen the existing experience,
not compete with it.

If a new idea introduces additional attention,
additional decisions,
or additional pressure,

the experience should be simplified before implementation continues.

Today is not designed to increase engagement.

It is designed to reduce cognitive load.

The best Today experience is the one that quietly disappears after helping the user reconnect with the present.

Every future decision about Today should answer:
